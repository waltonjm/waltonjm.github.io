Code Quality and Continuous Improvement

March 23, 2022 | Jacqueline Walton

Programming is a use or lose skill set. It is essential for any person dedicated enough to survive coding boot camp continue to improve their skills sets to ensure code quality. The following are some of the lessons learned:

  1. Maintain a Healthy and Safe Work Environment: Make sure you have an ergonomically correct work station. Check that your desk, keyboard, mouse, chair, monitor and lighting follow recommended ergonomic guidance. Invest in a standing desk or tabletop monitor stand that will raise to allow you to periodically stand throughout your coding activities. Keep your music down so you do not destroy your hearing. Lastly, if you have silicone sensitivity or allergy, make sure your headset are not coated in silicone or your in-ear headphones contain silicone. You may get ear infections, suffer from inflammation, skin rash etc.
  2. Coding Typing Test: Take 15 minutes every morning and take a coding typing test.
  3. Focus: Seriously, put your silicone-free headset on with your vibe music and get coding.
  4. Message: Someone always knows more than you. Slack, Discord...whatever; but just network and contribute to forums such as Stack Overflow.
  5. Read: Programming books, websites, community sites. Then read some more.
  6. Research: Question anything that does not make sense. Research MDN, Stack Overflow, Google for answers. Ask your friends or coworkers for help, if needed.
  7. Take Breaks: Take a shower, put on some clean clothes and go out with friends or family and just have fun. Because once you start --you cannot stop! If you do not become passionate about coding then you need to evaluate if coding is for you.
